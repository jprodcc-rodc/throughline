## SECTION 1 — Why this task exists (strategic context)

### The naming question is not "is Rodspan OK?"

The real question is structural:

> "Given Rodspan's strategic ambition (cross-model memory layer, anti-vendor
> stance, founder-driven brand, $X pricing, English-international Phase 1
> with EU expansion possible Phase 2-3), what naming choice maximizes
> 5-year strategic optionality and brand defensibility?"

Rodspan the current name has **known frictions**:

1. **Switzerland TM #677580** in software class
   - Current mitigation: geo-block EU
   - Strategic cost: closes EU expansion path without rename
2. **Pronunciation ambiguity**
   - 20-30% English speakers may hear "ROD-icks" rhyming with "dicks"
   - Current mitigation: pronunciation guide, founder video, written
     phonetic in posts
   - Strategic cost: low-grade brand friction at every introduction
3. **Coined word weight**
   - "Rodspan" carries no inherent meaning
   - Strategic cost: brand must earn meaning from scratch over years
4. **Founder anchor strength** (an asset, not friction)
   - Rod- (Rodc) → Rodspan is real founder fingerprint

### The core trade-off

- **Coined names** (Rodspan, Mem, Notion-coined-from-Latin) = blank slate,
  brand-able, high TM defensibility, low immediate resonance
- **Real word names** (Linear, Reflect, Granola) = instant resonance,
  weaker TM defensibility, harder to clear in 2026 saturated market
- **Hybrid names** (portmanteaux, Latin-rooted) = middle ground, harder
  to nail

Rodspan is currently in the coined category. The audit must evaluate:

1. Is Rodspan the strongest candidate **within the coined category**?
2. Does any **real-word or hybrid candidate** clearly beat coined Rodspan
   on multiple strategic dimensions?

### Outcome shapes

Three valid outcomes from this audit. Each carries strategic value:

- **Outcome A: Rodspan confirmed.** 75 candidates evaluated, no clear winner
  emerges. Rodc proceeds with Rodspan with full evidence base. The audit
  itself becomes part of the brand's intellectual provenance ("we did the
  rigorous work").
- **Outcome B: Marginal improvement found.** 1-2 candidates beat Rodspan on
  1-3 dimensions but don't dominate overall. Rodc decides based on
  strategic priorities (cost vs upside, risk vs status quo).
- **Outcome C: Strong winner found.** 1 candidate dominates Rodspan on 4+
  dimensions. Rodc seriously considers rename (cost: 4-6h ops + 2-4h
  brand book v2).

CC's job is to truth-track which outcome the evidence supports. Not bias
toward novelty. Not bias toward inertia. Bias toward **best strategic
position for Rodspan-the-product over the next 5 years**.

## SECTION 2 — Brand architect's framework (read before brainstorming)

### 2.1 — Strategic naming criteria (the 14 dimensions)

Every candidate (including Rodspan as benchmark) gets evaluated against
these 14 dimensions. This is the spine of the audit. Every dimension is
scored 1-5, with reasoning.

#### Operational dimensions (must-pass gates)

1. **Domain availability** (.app primary, .com bonus)
2. **Trademark clearance** (rough check, software class, EU + US + AU)
3. **App Store / Play Store collision** (productivity / AI / memory cat)
4. **Search engine clutter** (1st page Google for `"name" software`)

#### Linguistic / phonetic dimensions

5. **Pronunciation transparency** (can a stranger pronounce it correctly
   on first sight?)
6. **Spellability from sound** (can a stranger spell it correctly when
   heard?)
7. **Cross-cultural phonetic safety** (no rude meaning in EN/ES/FR/DE/
   ZH-Mandarin/JA — Rodspan's plausible markets)
8. **Sound symbolism / phonosemantics** (does the sound feel like the
   product? E.g., hard consonants for sharp/precise; soft for warm/safe)

#### Strategic / brand dimensions

9. **Archetype fit** (vs Rodspan's archetype from brand-book-v1.md)
10. **Voice fit** (does it pair naturally with Rodc's friends-intro
    voice — short paragraphs, parentheticals, anti-marketing?)
11. **Category positioning** (does it signal "thinking partner" vs "AI
    productivity tool" vs "note app"? Where does it land?)
12. **5-year strategic coherence** (in 2031 when LLMs are commodity, does
    this name still describe what Rodspan is?)
13. **Defensibility / uniqueness** (how hard is it for a competitor to
    create brand confusion? high uniqueness = low confusion risk)
14. **Founder + acquisition fit** (does it work in HN post titles, Twitter
    threads, video first-line; does Rodc — anonymous solo Asian founder
    — feel ownership of the name?)

### 2.2 — Senior naming-firm methodology (use this, not vibes)

A real naming workshop generates candidates across **multiple semantic
axes**. CC will use 7 axes, generate 10-12 candidates per axis, then
de-dupe and gate.

This produces ~75 unique candidates (some overlap expected).

#### The 7 generation axes

**Axis 1 — Continuity / thread / spine semantic field**
Words and roots: thread, spine, line, trail, stream, arc, weave, knit,
braid, carry, hold, keep
Latin/Greek: filum, linea, vector, taxis, nexus, rhema

**Axis 2 — Memory / recall / record semantic field**
Words and roots: memory, recall, mark, note, log, trace, cite, witness,
proof, receipt, record
Latin/Greek: memoria, mnemon, recordi, gravis, signum, codex

**Axis 3 — Light / clarity / transparency semantic field**
Words and roots: clear, plain, true, white, open, bright, frank, candid
Latin/Greek: claro, lumin, vera, lucid, aperta, candidus

**Axis 4 — Card / leaf / fold / page semantic field**
Words and roots: card, leaf, fold, page, sheet, slip, stack, deck,
quire, folio
Latin/Greek: folium, charta, scheda, pagina, codice

**Axis 5 — Sovereignty / ownership / yours semantic field**
Words and roots: own, mine, yours, hold, keep, vault, key, root,
ground, anchor
Latin/Greek: dominus, proprius, custos, fundus, radix

**Axis 6 — Spine of conversation / through-line semantic field**
(closest to Rodspan's actual mechanism — explicit in friends-intro)
Words and roots: through, across, between, after, since, after-, post-,
over-, mid-, span
Latin/Greek: trans-, inter-, per-, mediam

**Axis 7 — Founder anchor (Rod- / Rodc-derived)**
Rodc's pseudonym. The current Rodspan uses this. Generate 10 alternatives
keeping the Rod- anchor with different suffixes:
Rodway, Rodspan, Rodline, Rodweave, Rodanchor, Rodtrail, Rodare, Rodare,
Rodvale, Rodtide

### 2.3 — Generation rules (hard constraints)

Every candidate must pass these 8 hard rules before entering the candidate
list. Anything failing is silently dropped, not added with a flag.

1. ≤ 8 letters (memorability cap)
2. Pronounceable by English speakers without IPA
3. No `-dix` `-dex` `-dyks` suffixes (rod-DICKS adjacency + RØDE
   conflict)
4. No tech giant syllable cores (no "Apliz" "Goolex" "Mosoft")
5. Spellable from sound (skip if listening test fails)
6. No active known major competitor name (Mem / Reflect / Tana /
   Heptabase / Notion / Linear / Granola / Vellum / Spine / Throughline
   etc. are pre-eliminated)
7. No phonetic adjacency to slurs or rude words in EN/ES/FR/DE
8. Compatible with Rodspan's archetype (don't generate "Quirkpop" if
   archetype is Sage)

## SECTION 3 — Operational verification (Gates 1-4)

These are the elimination gates. Failing any one drops a candidate from
strategic evaluation. Run efficiently — most candidates will fail Gate 1.

### Gate 1 — Domain check

For each candidate (76 total including Rodspan benchmark):

- `{name}.app` — primary
- `{name}.com` — premium check
- `{name}.dev` — defensive check (lower priority)

**Method:** `web_fetch` registrar's whois (Namecheap, Porkbun, GoDaddy
all work). Do NOT rely on Google search alone.

**If automated check fails for a name:** mark `[CANNOT_VERIFY]`, attempt
through different registrar, do NOT hallucinate.

**Pass criteria:**
- ✅ `.app` available → minimum bar (clear)
- ✅ `.com` also available → "premium clear"
- ❌ both taken → eliminate (proceed to next candidate)

### Gate 2 — App Store / Play Store collision

For each candidate passing Gate 1:

**Method:** `web_search` query
`"{name}" site:apps.apple.com OR site:play.google.com`

**Pass criteria:**
- ✅ 0 apps with exact name → clean
- ⚠️ 1-2 apps in unrelated category (fishing, fashion, music) → tolerable
- ❌ Any app in productivity / AI / memory / note / chat / journaling →
  eliminate (high collision)

### Gate 3 — Web crowded check

For each candidate passing Gate 2:

**Method:** `web_search` 2 queries
- Query A: `"{name}" software OR product OR startup OR app`
- Query B: `"{name}" SaaS OR AI OR memory`

**Pass criteria:**
- ✅ < 5 product results, none in tech/SaaS → clean
- ⚠️ 5-10 results, but in unrelated industries → moderate (tolerable)
- ❌ 10+ results OR any tech-adjacent name collision → eliminate

### Gate 4 — Trademark rough check

For each candidate passing Gate 3:

**Method:**
1. `web_search` `"{name}" trademark EU OR European OR EUIPO`
2. `web_search` `"{name}" trademark United States OR USPTO`
3. `web_search` `"{name}" trademark Australia OR IP Australia`
   (since RØDE-style audio brands are Australia-clustered)
4. Attempt `web_fetch` https://www.tmdn.org/tmview/get-detail-by-search?text={name}
   (may not work — try)

**Pass criteria:**
- ✅ No TM hits in software class (9 / 42) in any of 3 jurisdictions →
  clean
- ⚠️ TM hits but in unrelated class (apparel, food, etc.) → tolerable
  (lawyer-confirmable)
- ❌ TM hits in software class → eliminate

**If automated check is impossible for a candidate:** mark
`[REQUIRES_LAWYER_CHECK]` and provisional advance to Section 4 — but flag
in final report that this candidate's TM status is not verified.
