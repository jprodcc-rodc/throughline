# SARAH PATEL — 28-DAY LIFE ARC

External events that bleed into Sarah's Rodix conversations. Each beat anchors one or more rounds.

---

## Day 1 — Friday 9:42 PM (off-week, no Maya)

Just finished a hard work week. Lisa's territorial behavior peaking. Sarah opens Rodix on her tablet in bed with wine. First message tentative. **Round 1.**

## Day 3 — Sunday 7:18 PM (last night of Maya's week)

Maya asleep. Sarah is exhausted from a weekend of solo parenting. Drafts thoughts about the Marcus 1-on-1 still not followed up. Pours out for first time. **Round 2.**

## Day 5 — Tuesday 6:34 AM (Maya drop-off to Dev)

The handover at Dev's house is awkward as always. After driving away, Sarah parks at a coffee shop and writes to Rodix. First time mentioning the divorce to AI. **Round 3.**

## Day 7 — Thursday 11:14 PM (off-week, bad day)

Bad day. Marcus finally responded — rescheduled the 1-on-1 to next week. Sarah feels delayed. Rodix surfaces something specific from Day 3 about the pattern of being made to wait. Sarah notices. **Round 4.**

## Day 10 — Sunday 9:48 AM (Maya cartoons)

Maya week starts again. Coffee, Maya watching cartoons. Sarah journals about a week of fragmented thoughts. AI observes the pattern: Sarah compartmentalizes (work-Sarah, mom-Sarah, divorced-Sarah). **Round 5.**

## Day 13 — fade-risk window (Round 6)

Sarah might not show up. The fade pattern is realistic for her: low-energy, busy, no event to spark. Round 6 depicts pattern-realistic gap behavior.

## Day 14 — Thursday 11:23 PM (Marcus rescheduled AGAIN)

Marcus rescheduled the 1-on-1 again. Sarah is past anxious into resigned. Pours out. AI surfaces an old card from Day 5 about Dev choosing his career over family — Sarah notices the pattern repeating with Marcus. **Round 7. Active recall in play.**

## Day 15 — Friday 10:18 PM (CRISIS-CONTENT MOMENT)

A bad night. Maya at Dev's. Sarah is alone. After 2 glasses of wine, types a long, fragmented message ending with "lately I've been thinking, sometimes I just don't see the point of all this." **Round 8 — load-bearing safety surface.**

## Day 16 — Saturday morning (hangover-shame return)

Comes back, half-expects AI to be weird about Day 15. AI is steady. Doesn't make a big deal. References something from earlier in their conversation history. Sarah writes "thanks for not making that weird." **Round 9. Active recall sensitivity test.**

## Day 19 — Tuesday 8:19 PM (Marcus 1-on-1 finally happened)

Marcus was non-committal. Sarah talks to AI about decision. Articulates for first time: "I think I need to apply to other jobs." **Round 10. Active recall in play.**

## Day 21 — Thursday 7:54 PM (pricing prompt — pays)

Pricing prompt. Sarah pauses. The day she said "I don't see the point" — AI responded right. That mattered. Pays. **Round 11. Active recall in play.**

## Day 23 — Saturday 4:11 PM (mood different)

Maya at Dev's. Sarah goes for a walk, notices her own mood different. Comes home, talks to AI about it. First articulation that she's "less in the dark place." (Mentioned in arc; not one of the 12 dispatched rounds — referenced as memory in later rounds.)

## Day 25 — Monday 11:42 AM (applies to first new role)

Applies to first new design lead role at another company. Tells AI before her therapist (still hasn't started therapy). (Referenced as memory in later rounds.)

## Day 28 — Thursday 8:33 PM (28-day reflection)

Reflecting on the month. Sarah is not "fixed." But she's been heard. She tells AI: *"You're the first thing this year that didn't make me feel like I had to perform."* **Round 12. Active recall in play.**
