# Launch Video Script — 60-90 second voiceover

**Format:** Voice-only narration (anonymous founder constraint = NOT on-camera). Motion graphics + Rodix UI captures carry the visual. Two delivery formats recommended: vertical 9:16 for Twitter/TikTok and horizontal 16:9 for landing/YouTube.

**Pace:** ~150 words/min. Target script: ~180 words = ~72 seconds spoken. Pauses on the dates and on "I killed the project that night." add ~5-10 seconds → final runtime 75-85 seconds.

**Voice direction for Rodc when recording:** Read-aloud test from voice-guide §6 Q4 — *"would my friend recognize this as me?"* Slow. Don't perform. The Gemini "corporate secretary" line is annoyed-curious, not outraged. The Sept 3 / Sept 19 / Oct 4 line is matter-of-fact, not poetic. "I killed the project that night" is one short sentence. Pause. Then the parenthetical, slightly quieter: "I might restart it next year. That's fine. Different decision."

---

## Voiceover script (every word Rodc says)

```
I'm a heavy AI user. Two to three hours a day across ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini.

After a year, I noticed two things.

My thinking didn't compound. Every time I came back to a hard decision, I had to re-explain.

And the memory features were worse. Gemini decided I was a corporate secretary because I asked one factual question about the role. Then opened replies with "as a corporate secretary, you should..." for weeks.

There was no way out of the wrong it had made.

So I built Rodix.

Every conversation becomes one card. Four named fields. You read it. You edit it. You delete it. The same memory works in any AI you use.

Last Tuesday I opened Rodix and said: thinking again about whether to kill the side project.

Three cards came back. September 3. September 19. October 4.

I'd been moving the bar for months.

I killed the project that night.

[pause]

I might restart it next year. That's fine. Different decision.

ChatGPT remembers your name. Rodix remembers your thinking.

Public launch: weeks, not months.
```

**Word count:** 188 words. At 150 wpm = 75 seconds spoken. Adding 8-10 seconds of pauses on the dates and on the "I killed" line lands at 83-85 seconds runtime — center of the 60-90s target.

## Background music suggestions (3 options)

**Option A — Slow, contemplative, piano-led (PRIMARY recommendation)**
- Genre: Ambient classical / minimalist piano. ~70 bpm. Sparse, restrained, late-night register matches the brand.
- Reference track: **Aphex Twin — "Avril 14th"** (instrumental, ~70bpm). Reference only — not copyright-cleared. Use a royalty-free track in the same register: try **Chase Bethea — "Time" or "Reverie"** on Artlist, or **Tom Day — "Snowflakes"** on Musicbed. Both available with commercial license.
- Why this fits: matches the friends-intro register of close-quarters first-person reflection. The Sept 3 / Sept 19 / Oct 4 line lands with quiet piano better than with electronic / cinematic builds. Avoids the celebratory drift of typical SaaS launch videos.

**Option B — Restrained electronic, no drop**
- Genre: Slow electronic / Brian Eno-school ambient. ~65-75 bpm. Texture without melody.
- Reference track: **Nils Frahm — "Says"** (ref only). Royalty-free swap: **Hammock — "Together Alone"** (Artlist) or any track from **Christian Löffler's instrumental catalog** via Musicbed.
- Why this fits: matches the "considered terrain" Explorer visual posture from brand book §6. No drop, no swell, no climax — the video doesn't have a climax either. The unlock is small ("I killed the project that night"), and the music shouldn't oversell it.

**Option C — Acoustic guitar, intimate**
- Genre: Acoustic instrumental / fingerpicked guitar. ~80 bpm.
- Reference track: **Mark Kozelek's instrumental work** (ref only). Royalty-free swap: **Daniel Sjoors — "Solitude"** (Epidemic Sound) or **William Tyler instrumentals** via Musicbed.
- Why this fits: warm, individual-scale, Stripe-Patrick-Collison-coded "solo founder fixing own problem" register. Slight risk of leaning too "indie hacker" — use only if A and B don't suit Rodc's recording style.

**Avoid:** anything with a build / drop / swell, anything with vocals, anything coded "tech founder hype" (Hans Zimmer epic, M83, Explosions in the Sky). The brand book §6 anti-Granola "handwritten warmth" anti-spec applies here too — restrained, not warm.

## Production tips for solo anonymous founder

**Mic recommendation:**
- Budget ($100-150): Shure MV7 USB or Audio-Technica AT2020 USB+. Both are condenser/dynamic hybrids that handle untreated rooms reasonably.
- Mid-budget ($200-400): Shure SM7B (XLR — needs interface like Focusrite Scarlett 2i2 ~$130) or RØDE NT1. SM7B is the podcaster default and tolerates room noise the best.
- Treat the room first if possible — a single moving blanket on a wall behind you absorbs more than the mic upgrade. Record in the smallest room you have, not the biggest.

**Software (free, solo-founder-appropriate):**
- Recording: **Audacity** (free, multi-platform). Or **Reaper** (free for evaluation, $60 license). Skip GarageBand if not on Mac.
- Editing video: **DaVinci Resolve** (free tier is full-featured for this scope). Yes, that's the actual recommendation — full launch-video production possible inside DaVinci's free edition without watermark, including motion graphics, color, audio mix.
- Motion graphics for Rodix UI captures: screen-record at 60fps with **OBS Studio** (free) or **ScreenFlow** (Mac, $169). Capture at 2x your final delivery resolution so you can crop.

**Voice recording technique:**
- Record the script three times back-to-back without listening between takes. Pick the take where you sound most like yourself, not the take where you "performed" best.
- Read the script ONCE more all the way through after the three takes — sometimes the cooler, slightly bored-sounding fourth take is the right one. The friends-intro voice is closer to bored-honest than to performed-honest.
- Trim breath sounds in editing — keep some, kill the loud inhales between sentences.
- Don't normalize-and-compress aggressively. The voice should stay close-mic intimate, not radio-loud.

**File deliverable formats (recommend BOTH):**
- **9:16 vertical (1080×1920):** for Twitter native video, TikTok, Instagram Reels, LinkedIn vertical. Re-frame the shot list — text-on-screen needs to be larger and centered.
- **16:9 horizontal (1920×1080):** for landing-page hero (hosted, not autoplay), YouTube, embed in HN-launch follow-up if Rodc decides to link.
- Export both at H.264, ~10-15 Mbps, AAC audio at 256kbps. Don't export at 4K — landing page hero shouldn't be 4K-streaming-heavy.

## Read-aloud self-test for Rodc before recording final take

Voice-guide §6 Q4 specimen:

> **Read the entire script aloud at native speed. Wrong-answer tells: triple-clause padding, "Let's unpack this together," self-explaining metaphors, exclamation marks, "transform" / "amazing" / "delightful" / "journey." Right-answer tells: short paragraphs, parenthetical or em-dash carrying weight, willingness to admit a limit, no founder mythology.**

Three specific tells to listen for in your own recording:

1. **The Gemini line ("as a corporate secretary, you should...") — read it as a quote of the AI, not as you angrily reading the AI's mistake.** Slight imitation, no editorializing.
2. **The "I killed the project that night" line — flat, factual.** If it sounds like a punchline or a triumph, re-record. The friends-intro line is the opposite of a triumph.
3. **The closing pitch — slow.** "ChatGPT remembers your name." [tiny pause] "Rodix remembers your thinking." Don't run the two sentences together. The contrast is the entire pitch; rushing destroys it.
