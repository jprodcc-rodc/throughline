## Challenge 3 — Spin red flags. Pick any sentence that sounds marketing-y rather than founder-y.

**Weakest sentence (PH launch package, tagline):** *"Cross-model AI memory. White-box cards. Real export."*

**Quote:** the literal tagline.

**1-sentence reasoning:** this sentence is the closest any deliverable comes to a feature-list adjective stack — three short noun phrases that pattern-match to SaaS taglines ("powerful, beautiful, intuitive"); the only thing saving it from full spin is each phrase corresponds to one of the four architectural bets and uses brand-book-locked vocabulary ("white-box" not "transparent", "real export" not "data portability"). I considered replacing with *"Memory you can read, edit, and take with you"* but rejected that because the friends-intro register is "declares mechanism" not "describes user benefit" — and the PH tagline ceiling (60 chars) doesn't support a friends-intro sentence at full register. **Verdict:** acceptable spin-edge given platform constraint, but it's the closest call across all five channels.

**Other near-misses considered:**
- HN closing line ("That's the bet.") — close to drama but earns its place because the preceding paragraph laid the structural argument; "that's the bet" is the Stripe-Patrick-Collison register, not LinkedIn founder-coach register.
- Founder essay paragraph 11 ("None of that is a feature list; it is a posture.") — this teeters; "posture" is abstract. Saved by the immediate concrete unpack ("The bet is that the memory belongs to the user, the model is interchangeable, and everything else falls out of that") which grounds the abstraction.
