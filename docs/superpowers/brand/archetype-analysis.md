## 1. Primary archetype: **Explorer**

The defining quality is independence — Rodspan exists so the user's thinking is not captured, classified, or rented. Every value the friends-intro encodes points the same direction: portability over platform, evidence over credentials, "different decision" over commitment-as-identity. Rodspan's relationship to its user is not a wise mentor's (Sage), nor a healer's (Caregiver), nor a revolutionary's (Rebel). It is *fellow traveler who has already walked the AI-tooling frontier and built the gear they wished existed*. The voice is not pitching from a podium; it is comparing notes at a campfire.

**Evidence 1 — the 4-fold critique reads as a sovereignty manifesto, not a wisdom thesis.** The friends-intro lists what is broken about ChatGPT memory, Gemini personalization, Claude projects: "**Black-box tags.** You can't see what they 'remember' about you. **Wrong, and you can't fix it.** ... **Locked to one vendor.** Your ChatGPT memory means nothing in Claude. ... **Not actually yours.** No real export." Each indictment names a specific way the user has been *contained*: cannot see, cannot edit, cannot leave, cannot extract. The matching commitments — "White-box thinking cards. Cross-model. Active recall. Real export. One click, markdown files. Your hard drive. Your call." — are framed as *exits*, not as illumination (Sage) or transformation (Magician). The pivot sentence is the giveaway: "the memory belongs to the user, the model is interchangeable." Not a Sage's epistemic claim about how memory works — an Explorer's claim about who owns the road and who is just renting horses.

**Evidence 2 — the "Gemini decided I was a corporate secretary" passage is the Explorer's signature wound.** The friends-intro: "Gemini decided I was a corporate secretary because I asked one factual question about the role. Then it kept opening replies with 'as a corporate secretary, you should...' for weeks. There's no way to debug that. No source attribution. No real edit." The reading guide asked which archetype reacts to mis-categorization with this specific frustration — not anger, not contempt, but "this was avoidable." Caregiver would feel hurt ("they didn't see me"); Sage would feel intellectually offended ("the inference is incorrect"); Rebel would be furious ("they have no right"). Rodc's reaction is none of these. It is the reaction of someone who walked into a room and the door locked behind him: flat recognition that *the architecture made escape impossible by design*. The Explorer's defining nightmare is being classified into a box from which one cannot exit. "There's no way to debug that" is not "they were wrong about me" — it is "I cannot get out of the wrong they made about me." The product Rodspan builds in response is escape infrastructure: every label editable, every card deletable, every export one click.

**Evidence 3 — "killed the project that night. (I might restart it next year. That's fine. Different decision.)" is Explorer cadence in pure form.** Read the parenthetical. No triumph (rejecting Hero), no extracted moral (rejecting Sage), no resolution (rejecting Caregiver), no manifesto (rejecting Rebel). The Explorer treats decisions as paths, not oaths: kill the project is not heroic; restart is not failure; both are different decisions on different days. Compare a Sage-Rodc closing: "Reading the cards, I realized I had been avoiding the decision — the lesson is that the question itself was the answer." Compare a Hero-Rodc: "I made the hard call that night." Rodc writes neither. He writes "I killed the project that night," then a parenthetical that defuses the gravity. The product mechanism (cards revealing the moving-bar pattern) is presented matter-of-factly: "That's the kind of insight you can't get from any one conversation. You can only see it from the spine of conversations." Rodspan is a tool for traveling well through one's own thinking — not for arriving at wisdom.

**Evidence 4 — the honest-status section refuses every available founder mythology.** "Founder: solo, anonymous, working out of Asia, second half of a multi-year build." Each clause is a refusal: *solo* refuses team-credential, *anonymous* refuses personal-brand-credential, *working out of Asia* refuses Silicon-Valley-credential, *second half of a multi-year build* refuses both stealth-mystery and launch-urgency. This is where Everyman shading becomes clearest: "I'm a heavy AI user. ~2-3 hours a day, across ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, plus a few more. After a year of this, I noticed two things." The "why me" is *time on the road*, not expertise. The Explorer's claim to authority is always travel-mileage. Critically: "If you ask AI 'what's a good restaurant in Lisbon' twice a week and that's it, you don't need this. ChatGPT's fine." Explorer self-selection — Rodspan is for fellow travelers, not for everyone. The Sage offers wisdom to all who will listen; the Explorer maps territory for those who will walk it.

## 2. Secondary archetype: **Everyman** (color, not core)

Everyman colors the Explorer primary at the *register* of voice — peer-to-peer, anti-elite, no expertise claim. The friends-intro grounds itself in shared user experience: "Re-explained a project to ChatGPT for the fifth time and felt the soul-tax. Used three AIs for different things and wished they shared a brain. Tried 'AI memory' features and got mildly creeped out by what they assumed." That is Everyman solidarity — *I am you, we have noticed the same things*. The closing tagline "ChatGPT remembers your name. Rodspan remembers your thinking" speaks across the table, not down from a stage. If Everyman were primary, Rodspan would tilt toward Mailchimp "small-business-friendly" warmth or Trello "for the rest of us" populism — both wrong, since Rodspan is not democratizing a category, it is offering a narrow architectural alternative for a self-selected user. Everyman as color means: speak as peer, refuse expertise theater, never lecture. Kept secondary, Everyman keeps Explorer's authority warm rather than aloof.

## 3. Three archetypes explicitly rejected

### Rejected: Sage (engaging with v1's verdict and the reading guide's explicit rejection)

The reading guide flagged Sage as "too clean" and v1 still landed there because it read the *internal* mechanics of the AI in product (system prompt, claim-extractor null-default, round-3 reflection rule) as if those were the brand's archetype. They are the *AI character's* archetype within the product — Rodspan-the-AI-companion does adopt Socratic restraint when the user is mid-thinking, and that internal posture is correctly Sage-flavored. But the *brand's* archetype is the stance Rodc takes when standing next to Rodspan and saying "here is what I built and why." That stance is not Sage. There is no claim to wisdom in the friends-intro. There is no "let me help you understand AI memory." The closest move is the cards-revealing-the-pattern example, and even there Rodc is explicit that *the user, not Rodspan, sees the pattern*: "Reading those three cards back-to-back, the pattern is suddenly obvious." Rodspan is not the wise mentor handing down insight; it is the rigging that lets the user see their own pattern. Sage products tend toward sanctimony ("understand yourself"); Rodc's voice has none of that — "Different decision. That's fine" is the opposite of a Sage punchline. **The internal AI's Sage posture is a product-mechanism feature, not the brand. Brand archetype operates one layer up.**

### Rejected: Rebel

Rebel is genuinely tempting. The 4-fold critique is structurally a manifesto-against-incumbents: "Vendors don't actually want you to own your AI memory ... So they keep memory locked, opaque, and uneditable — by design." That is a Rebel sentence in shape. But the *register* is wrong. Rebel's defining quality is outrage; Rodc's is matter-of-fact recognition. Rebel says "this is broken and I am here to break it"; Rodc says "this is broken by design, here is an architecture that exits the design." Rebel builds revolutions; Rodc built an alternative. The closing tells the story: a Rebel would end with a call to arms ("delete your ChatGPT memory tonight"); Rodc ends with "If you want early access, ping me. If you want to wait for public launch, also fine — I'll post when it's open." The "also fine" is non-Rebel by definition. The intro names ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude specifically and critiques them architecturally — but does not mock them, and explicitly recommends ChatGPT for restaurant queries. Not Rebel posture. A traveler's review of available routes — some good for some trips, none good for the trip Rodspan is built for.

### Rejected: Magician

Magician is the lazy default for any AI product, and Rodspan declines it precisely and architecturally. The friends-intro never uses transformation language. Cards do not "magically" appear; they "extract" via a pipeline Rodc describes plainly: "an intent classifier ... a claim extractor — pulls topic / concern / hope / question. Both with explicit accuracy gates and human-evaluated test sets, not vibes." The "not vibes" is anti-Magician in spirit and syntax. Active recall is searching past cards via embeddings before the AI generates — a procedure, not an enchantment. `claim_extractor.md` names invention as a *critical failure* and returns null rather than fabricate. Magician's defining failure mode is charlatanry — claiming transformative power one cannot deliver; Rodspan's pipeline is engineered to never make that claim. Even the recall-card label "I brought this back" is relational (fellow-traveler returning a forgotten thing) rather than magical. The brainstorm doc lock is explicit: "trust-evoking > wow-evoking" (Rodc 2026-05-01). Magician's whole register is wow.

## 4. Implications

**Visual identity.** The locked palette already fits. Warm dark `#18181b` + amber `#d97706` reads as campfire-at-night gear-shop — not Sage's white-paper austerity nor Magician's gradient theater. Inter at restrained weights, Lucide line icons at 1.5-1.8px stroke, no emoji adornments. Whitespace breathes but never preaches. Imagery (if used) favors terrain and tools over concept-art. Anthropic + Linear remain right tonal references — not because they are Sage-clean but because they wear independence visibly.

**Voice / cadence / register.** Short paragraphs, parentheticals that defuse rather than dramatize, em-dashes for emphasis, lists only when they map to discrete items. Refusal of marketing language is the load-bearing pattern: "Not 'better AI'", "not vendor lock-in", "Not a journaling app." The negation pattern is Explorer pre-trip route-elimination — the writer has done the work of figuring out what this is *not* before claiming what it is. Honest qualification — "weeks, not months", "we can't promise zero-knowledge — that would be a lie given the architecture" — is the brand's load-bearing trust signal. Sycophancy, hedge-padding, triumph all out.

**Messaging / hero copy.** Hero ≤ 12 words; subheadline ≤ 30 words. The friends-intro tagline already nails it: "**Memory layer for AI chat. Cross-model. White-box. Yours.**" Each clause encodes an Explorer value (independence, mobility, transparency, ownership) without naming it. The shorter pitch — "ChatGPT remembers your name. Rodspan remembers your thinking." — is Everyman peer-to-peer cadence applied to an Explorer claim. Avoid "powerful," "robust," "delightful," "10x," "transform," "supercharge," "magical," "seamless." Prefer architectural verbs (extract, surface, export, switch, leave).

**Customer relationship.** Not impressed (Magician). Not soothed (Caregiver). Not enlightened (Sage). Not mobilized (Rebel). *Equipped.* The successful Rodspan moment is the user thinking "huh, I have my thinking with me now — the model is interchangeable, the memory is mine, I can leave whenever." Trust accumulates through consistency of restraint and visible reality of exit (export button, markdown files, editability of every card). Relationship: fellow-traveler-with-better-gear, not student-with-mentor.

## 5. Anti-implications (avoid drift to Explorer's failure modes)

Explorer does NOT mean *aimless or commitment-phobic*. The failure modes are aimlessness and alienation. Rodspan must not slide into "leave any time, never settle" posture; the export button is a guarantee, not an invitation to abandon. The brand encodes *freedom to leave* as the foundation of *willingness to invest*. Healthy Explorer says: "stay as long as it serves you, leave with everything you came with."

Explorer does NOT mean *frontiersman cowboy / lone-wolf founder mythology*. The Everyman color prevents this drift. Rodc is solo, anonymous, working out of Asia — but the voice is "a heavy AI user who noticed two things," not "rugged individualist who needs no one." Evidence-not-credentials keeps Explorer from drifting toward Hero individualism.

Explorer does NOT mean *travel as theme*. No map iconography, no compass metaphors, no "your journey" copy. Explorer is a *posture toward freedom*, not a *visual genre*. The visual system should look like restrained software (Anthropic / Linear restraint), not a hiking outfitter.

Explorer does NOT mean *anti-establishment posturing*. Rebel-drift is the most dangerous adjacent failure. Rodspan critiques incumbents specifically and architecturally; it does not perform contempt. The frame is exit, not battle. "Cross-model" is a product property, not a slogan against OpenAI. Any "vs. them" frame in marketing copy has drifted from Explorer to Rebel and must be rewritten.

Explorer does NOT mean *the AI inside the product is also an Explorer*. Brand archetype operates at the Rodc-presents-Rodspan layer. The AI-companion-inside-Rodspan is a different character — a Sage-flavored Socratic interlocutor (per `rodspan_system.md`). This split is intentional and load-bearing: brand promises freedom of movement through your thinking; in-product AI offers Socratic restraint within that thinking. Voice-extractor and position-strategist must preserve both layers.

## 6. Reconciliation with prior v1 (Sage primary)

v1 (`archetype-analysis-brainstorm-based.md`) concluded Sage primary, Explorer secondary. The conclusion was internally well-reasoned but built on *internal product mechanics* (system prompt, claim-extractor null-default, round-3 reflection rule). v1 correctly identified Sage as the AI-character's posture inside the product. The error was layer-collapse: treating the in-product AI's archetype as the brand's.

Three friends-intro signals shift the verdict:

1. **The 4-fold critique is architectural escape, not illumination.** v1 cited `claim_extractor.md`'s null-default as Sage epistemic humility; v2 reads the same rule as Explorer's anti-fabrication discipline (Explorer refuses to invent territory; Sage withholds claim until certain). Both readings are coherent in isolation — but "the memory belongs to the user, the model is interchangeable" is a sovereignty statement, not a Sage thesis.

2. **The "Gemini corporate secretary" passage is the Explorer's signature wound.** v1 did not have this passage as primary input. The flat recognition of a categorization-trap with no escape is Explorer-defining. Sage would diagnose the categorization as inferentially incorrect; Explorer names it as *unescapable* and builds the escape.

3. **"Killed the project / different decision / fine" cadence is incompatible with Sage's wisdom-extraction reflex.** The in-product round-3 synthesis rule is Sage signature for that AI character — but the brand-voice example Rodc gives deliberately *refuses* synthesis. No extracted lesson, no punchline. Sage cannot help itself from the moral; Explorer leaves the road open.

v1 is not wrong about the in-product AI's Sage flavoring — that remains useful for `rodspan_system.md` work and the brand-voice-extractor's prompt-voice section. v2 reads the *brand* archetype one layer up: Rodc's stance presenting the product. Explorer with Everyman color.

Handoff: brand-voice-extractor should capture *Rodc's* register (refusal-of-marketing, parenthetical asides, matter-of-fact decisions, peer-to-peer specifics) for landing copy, founder essay, HN post, Twitter thread. The in-product AI voice in `rodspan_system.md` is preserved as *product-character voice* distinct from brand voice. Position-strategist foregrounds sovereignty / portability / anti-lock-in (Explorer) over wisdom / pattern-recognition (Sage), even though the mechanism delivers pattern-recognition as side benefit.

## 7. Type-A escalation note

**Mild escalation flagged for Rodc.** v1 → Sage; v2 → Explorer. The verdict has shifted and contradicts the v1 brand-book that downstream subagents may already have consumed. Rodc should see the shift and reasoning before final sign-off.

The shift does not contradict the in-product AI's character. System prompt, claim-extractor discipline, round-3 reflection rule — all remain correctly Sage-flavored for the AI-companion *inside* Rodspan. What changes is the brand archetype: Rodc-the-founder's stance when introducing the product. The friends-intro encodes Explorer values (sovereignty, portability, evidence-not-credentials, decisions-as-paths) more cleanly than Sage values, and the reading guide explicitly steered v2 away from Sage with the "too clean" rejection. v2 honored the steer and found body evidence supports it.

If Rodc disagrees, the productive disagreement-frame is: *"is the brand's primary posture freedom-of-movement (Explorer) or wise-companionship (Sage)?"* Both archetypes share secondary qualities (restraint, refusal of theater, matter-of-fact register), so much operational guidance — visual system, anti-buzzword discipline, Anthropic + Linear references — survives either verdict. What changes is the *frame*: Explorer-Rodspan sells exit and ownership ("your thinking, portable, yours"); Sage-Rodspan sells insight and clarity ("see the pattern in your own thinking"). The friends-intro foregrounds the first.

Flagged as escalation, not escalation-required: downstream voice-extractor and position-strategist can proceed with v2; the verdict is recoverable if Rodc returns to Sage. But the verdict-shift deserves Rodc's eye before brand-book integration.
