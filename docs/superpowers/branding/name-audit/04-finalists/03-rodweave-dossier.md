## Etymology and meaning

Coined compound: **Rod** (founder pseudonym) + **weave** (EN word: to interlace threads, by extension to construct/knit-together). Carries thread-of-thought metaphor.

**⚠️ Gap 1 finding (Rodc STOP 3.5 stress test):** "Rod weave" is a documented technical term in textile production. From Britannica + Wikipedia + textile industry sources:

> *"Rod weave: in warp-pile fabrics, a rod is inserted through the entire width of the web when pile warps are raised. The round rod creates velvet loops; the grooved rod creates cut-pile velvet by running a knife down the groove. Rod weaving requires meticulous attention; an average velvet weaver produces 20-25 cm of fabric per day."*

This is a real industry term, not a coincidence. Anyone with textile / craft / fashion background hearing "Rodweave" will pattern-match to "rod weave" (pile fabric weaving).
