## 1. The strategic question and how I framed it

The audit was not asking *"is Rodix OK?"* — it was asking, structurally:

> *"Given Rodix's strategic ambition (cross-model AI memory, anti-vendor stance, founder-driven brand, English-international Phase 1 with EU expansion possible Phase 2-3), what naming choice maximizes 5-year strategic optionality and brand defensibility?"*

The framework: 14 dimensions × 76 candidates (75 alternatives + Rodix benchmark) is the truth-tracking apparatus. Section 9 anti-bias safeguards required me to actively resist both novelty bias (find-a-winner) and inertia bias (protect-Rodix). Both were tested against in stress-test pushback at STOP 3.

Three valid outcomes were possible:

- **A.** Rodix confirmed (no clear winner).
- **B.** Marginal improvement (1-2 candidates beat on 1-3 dims).
- **C.** Strong winner (1 candidate dominates on 4+ dims).

The audit landed at **B/C borderline**: Rodspan beats Rodix on 6 dims at ≥1-point gap (close to C territory) but rename economics + context factors keep the decision genuinely open (close to B framing).

## 2. Methodology

**Generation (Section 2):** 7 semantic axes × 10-12 candidates per axis. Hard rules per task spec §2.3 applied at generation (≤8 letters / pronounceable / no -dix/-dex/-dyks suffix / no tech-giant cores / spellable / no major competitor / no slur adjacency / Explorer+Everyman archetype fit). Final pool: 75 alternatives + Rodix = 76.

**Operational gates (Section 3):** Staged. Gate 1 (`.app` + `.com`) on all 76 via RDAP HTTP query (rdap.org for `.app` via Google Registry redirect; rdap.verisign.com for `.com`). Gates 2-4 (App Store / web crowded / TM rough check) on Gate-1 survivors via WebSearch. **62 candidates eliminated at Gate 1** (both `.app` and `.com` registered — .app market is ~98% saturated for short EN/Latin coined names by 2026). 1 eliminated at Gate 2 (`domain.app` — heavy productivity-app collision).

**Strategic evaluation (Section 4):** 10 dimensions × 14 op-clear candidates. Each cell scored 1-5 with reasoning. Weighting per task spec §6.1 with Rodc STOP 2 boosts on dim 11 (5) + dim 12 (6) to capture .com brand-spoof / 5-year ops costs.

**Stress-test re-scoring (Section 4 v2):** After STOP 3, Rodc identified 6 gaps in pre-stress scoring (rubric overfit on .com weighting; missing compound-vs-coined cognitive packaging; empty-coined optionality reframe; honest rename cost; RØDE X uniform application; voice-fit 5-test execution). All 6 applied; scoring re-run honestly.

**Decision matrix + sensitivity (Section 6):** 5 weighting schemes tested for robustness. Rodspan held rank 1 across all 5.

**Limitations:** TM check is web-search rough; lawyer-grade clearance recommended for any finalist before commit. Domain availability may flicker (registrar parking dynamics). Cross-cultural check is desk-research only, not native-speaker focus group.

Rewriting these artifacts honestly under a new name takes substantive editorial work, not mechanical substitution.

### 5.2 — Phase 1 alpha launch is on a 21-30 day critical path

Rodc-context.md notes US LLC registration in progress (1-4 week lead time per third-party agent), Paddle merchant approval (5-7 days), Privacy Policy lawyer-spot-consult (3-10 days), DNS propagation (1-3 days), domain checkout pending Porkbun (HSTS preload notice page), buffer for unknowns. A rename adds:

- **+1-2 weeks** brand-artifact rewrite + Rodc review
- **+1-2 weeks** Rodc mental adaptation to live with new name in marketing

This pushes Phase 1 alpha launch to **45-60+ days** instead of 21-30. For a solo-founder bootstrapped product where calendar velocity matters (alpha cohort momentum, public-launch HN timing window, year-end posture), a 3-4 week launch delay is non-trivial.

### 5.3 — Empty-coined dominates the iconic SaaS-naming base rate

Empty-coined / opaque / re-purposed-real-word names dominate top-tier modern SaaS:

- **Empty-coined:** Stripe (originally meant card-stripe; pivoted to embedded finance without renaming), Notion, Linear, Vercel, Figma, Slack (one-word repurposed), Mercury, Plaid, Brex, Anthropic, Spotify, Snowflake, Databricks, Datadog, Replit, Cursor, GitHub, Coinbase, Twilio.
- **Compound-coined:** Mailchimp, HubSpot, Salesforce, Workday, Zendesk, Mailgun, BlueJeans — these are rarer and tend toward utility-flavor (mail-chimp is a literal compound; sales-force describes the user). Few iconic compound brands.

The base-rate signal: founder-led tech startups that became iconic brands gravitated toward empty-coined names. The reasons (per branding theory): empty names accumulate meaning *as the company grows*, are pivot-friendly, are easier to defend as TM, and feel premium / company-shaped rather than tool/feature-shaped.

**Rodspan is the unconventional bet.** Compound-coined → utility-flavor → tool-shaped vs company-shaped. The name *describes a feature* (spans your thinking) rather than *represents the company*. This is not necessarily wrong (Mailchimp is a billion-dollar brand) but it's against the SaaS-naming base rate that suggests Rodix's empty-coined shape is the more conventional / lower-risk choice for the next decade of brand growth.

### 5.4 — How these context factors interact with the score

The score (Rodspan +25%) is a snapshot of operational + linguistic + strategic strengths. The context factors point at *cost of acting on the score in May 2026 specifically*:

- Wave 1c just shipped → switching cost is at peak right now (all artifacts fresh)
- LLC critical path → calendar cost is highest right now (paperwork in flight)
- Empty-coined base rate → strategic cost of switching to a non-base-rate name shape

If the audit had run **3 months ago** (before Wave 1c, before LLC initiation), context factors 1+2 would weigh much less, and the score's Rodspan-favoring verdict would be more clearly actionable. If the audit had run **3 months from now** (post-launch, post-LLC), factor 2 would weigh less but factor 1 would weigh even more (more launched-on-Rodix artifacts to redo).

The audit's timing relative to Rodc's launch calendar is a real input. Score + context together is the honest picture.

### Reading B — "Rodix retains" is supported

- **Wave 1c just shipped on Rodix** (5.1). Switching cost is at peak right now; ~30-50h CC + Rodc-mental + LLC delay + sunk cost.
- **LLC critical path** (5.2) — rename adds 1-4 weeks to Phase 1 launch calendar at exactly the wrong time.
- **Empty-coined SaaS base rate** (5.3) — Stripe / Notion / Linear / Vercel / Figma / Slack / Mercury / Plaid / Brex all empty-coined. Compound-coined is rarer + utility-flavored.
- Rodix's empty-coined optionality is a real pivot-friendly asset across architectural shifts in 2027-2031.

- Zero web/app-store collisions for "Rodix" exact name → strong defensibility moat.
- The +25% Rodspan margin shrinks meaningfully under residual gaps (see §9 — limitations) toward the marginal/tied zone where Rodc's threshold says inertia wins.

### Decision framework Rodc may use

- **If you weight the score more heavily** (data-tracking, brand quality compounds over 5 years, the 21-30 day delay is acceptable for the right name): **Rodspan**.
- **If you weight the context more heavily** (Wave 1c just shipped, launch calendar matters, empty-coined is the proven SaaS base rate): **Rodix**.

Both are defensible. Neither is wrong. The audit cannot make this call — Rodc's strategic priorities (launch velocity vs brand optimization, base-rate conformity vs distinctiveness, sunk-cost discounting style) determine the answer.

**My honest read** (offered as one input, not a verdict): the score gap is meaningful but the context factors are also meaningful. ## 9. Limitations and unknowns

**Two residual rubric gaps Rodc identified (do NOT re-score; document for honesty):**

1. **Dim 7 cross-cultural may over-credit Rodspan.** Rodix scored 3 on dim 7 (mild rod-DICKS EN adjacency); ZH/JA/ES/FR/DE all clean. Rodspan scored 5. The Rodix issue is strictly EN-monolingual; the Rodspan advantage on dim 7 is partly artifactual since the only meaningful gap is EN-pronunciation. If Rodix's dim 7 were re-scored to 4 (acknowledging only EN rod-DICKS issue, not full cross-cultural failure), the dim 7 gap shrinks from +2 to +1 and the +25% margin becomes ~+22%. Still material but closer to borderline.

2. **Dim 9 empty-coined +1 may under-credit base-rate signal.** The audit gave Rodix +1 on dim 9 for empty-coined optionality. The 5.3 base-rate analysis suggests this credit is *under*-stated — empty-coined dominates iconic SaaS at base-rate level (9 of the 10 referenced examples in 5.3 are empty-coined). If Rodix's dim 9 were re-scored to 5 (full base-rate match), Rodix gains +4 weighted and the +25% margin becomes ~+22%. Combining with (1) above, +20% in worst-case.

Either residual gap, alone or combined, **does not flip the verdict** — Rodspan still materially outperforms — but they narrow the margin and reinforce that the decision is genuinely close.

**Other limitations:**

3. **TM check is web-search rough, not lawyer-grade.** Any finalist (Rodix included) needs lawyer-grade clearance across EUIPO / USPTO / IP Australia / Swiss IGE before brand commitment. `[REQUIRES_LAWYER_CHECK]` flag applies to all 14 op-clear candidates.
4. **App Store / Play Store search may have gaps.** Apps published in non-EN markets may not surface in WebSearch. International app-store browse would be more thorough.
5. **Cross-cultural check is desk-research only.** A 3-5 person native-speaker focus group across ZH-Mandarin / JA / ES / FR / DE would be more authoritative on dim 7. 1-2h work, $0 cost via Rodc network.
6. **Pronunciation risk modeled, not user-tested.** A 5-person blind pronunciation test on Rodix vs Rodspan vs Rodkeep would be definitive on dim 5 (~1h, $0 cost via Rodc network).
7. **CH TM #677580 status not independently verified today.** I trusted rodc-context.md's note. A direct Swiss IGE database check would be authoritative.
8. **Compound-vs-empty-coined cognitive packaging is treated as a sub-dimension** (Gap 2 stress test) but is not a fully-developed dimension in the audit framework. Brand-architect literature has more nuanced treatment than I applied; my application is heuristic.
9. **The +25% margin is robust across the sensitivity schemes I tested**, but weighting choices upstream (e.g., dim 11+12 boost reflecting .com ops cost) shaped the result. A fully different weighting philosophy could land differently.
