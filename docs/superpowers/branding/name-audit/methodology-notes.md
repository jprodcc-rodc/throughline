## 1. Spec-vs-brand-book reconciliation: archetype anchor

**Conflict:** Task spec Section 13 example uses *"Sage archetype's clarity-and-precision register"* as illustrative. `brand-book-v1.md` §4 (locked 2026-05-03 by Rodc, friends-intro-canonical) reads:

> **Primary: Explorer. Secondary: Everyman (color, not core).**
> The defining quality is sovereignty.

**Resolution (Rodc-confirmed STOP 0):** The audit anchors on **Explorer (primary) + Everyman (color)**, not Sage. Dimension 9 (Archetype fit) scores every candidate against sovereignty + anti-lock-in + equipped-not-impressed posture, NOT against epistemic-clarity-and-precision.

**Why the spec example was Sage:** the task was authored before brand-book-v1.md was finalized. The Sage example is a writing illustration of *how to score with specificity* (hard-consonant onset → archetype fit), not a constraint on which archetype to use.

**Implication for scoring:** candidates that lean Sage (epistemic-clarity / wisdom / precision-for-its-own-sake — e.g., *Veritas*, *Lucid*, *Candor*) score lower on dim 9 than candidates that lean Explorer (sovereignty / freedom-to-leave / spine-of-thought — e.g., *Strand*, *Custos*, *Rodspan*). Light-semantic candidates can score well on dim 9 IF they bind to user-sovereignty rather than philosophical-clarity (e.g., *transparent-because-the-user-can-see* = Explorer-coded; *transparent-because-truth-is-good* = Sage-coded).

**Anti-implications I'm watching for** (per brand-book-v1.md §4 anti-implications):
- Explorer drift to *travel/journey/cowboy* visual genre — flagged on Rodway, Rodtrail, Rodvale, Trail, Path, Vado at axis-generation time
- Explorer drift to *anti-establishment posturing* — Rodix critiques incumbents architecturally, never mocks
- Explorer drift to *commitment-phobic / aimless* — sovereignty is the foundation of investment, not the absence of it

## 5. Hard-rules pre-check at generation (Section 2.3 application)

All 75 candidates passed Section 2.3's 8 hard rules at generation time. Specific decisions:

- **Rule 3 (-dix/-dex/-dyks suffix ban).** I read the rule strictly to mean those exact suffixes. *Cordis* (ends -dis) and *Codice* (ends -ce, English-pron -dis) are kept since -dis ≠ -dix. Both flagged for cross-cultural sound check at Section 4.
- **Rule 5 (spellable from sound).** *Mnemo* / *Mneme* (silent m, Greek mnemon) carry spelling-risk flags; included because the silent-m has cultural recognition (mnemonic). Will be honestly scored on dim 6.
- **Rule 6 (pre-eliminated competitors).** Cross-checked all 75 against the named-out list: Mem / Reflect / Tana / Heptabase / Notion / Linear / Granola / Vellum / Spine / Throughline. None match. Also defensively excluded: *Mem-* prefixes that read as "Mem.ai".
- **Rule 8 (archetype fit Explorer+Everyman).** Travel-drift candidates (Rodway, Rodtrail, Rodvale, Trail, Path, Vado) are flagged but kept — Rodc may want to see them surface to confirm the rejection. Sage-leaning light-clarity candidates (Veritas, Lucid, Candor, Lucent) are kept because Explorer-coded transparency (user-sovereignty visibility) is brand-coherent.

## 7. Reusable patterns applied

This audit applies five patterns from `docs/superpowers/tonight/reusable-patterns.md`:

- **Type-A surfacing with verbatim candidates** — every candidate gets verbatim spelling + IPA + semantic gloss, not abstract description; finalists get 3 verbatim sample-sentence integrations.
- **3-actor model** — Type-A items per Section 12 (final pick / rename economics / strategic priority) → Rodc only; Type-B (per-candidate scoring) → CC judgment with documented reasoning; Type-C (red flags) → STOP escalation.
- **Honest senior-consultant revisit** — after first-pass scoring, top picks are stress-tested against Rodc-named integration scenarios (HN post / Twitter bio / video opener) before the recommendation memo.
- **Persona-coverage matrix** — Section 6 decision matrix is a 14-dim × top-5-candidate grid with per-cell verdict.
- **Voice-fit 5-test as scorecard** — dim 10 sub-scored on Specific / Anti-spin / Refuses-to-dramatize / Could Rodc write this? / Not Caregiver.
