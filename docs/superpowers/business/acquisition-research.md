## Source 1 — Show HN front-page (2024–2025)

Front-page Show HN typically delivers ~10,000 visitors over 6–12 hours; landing→signup spread is wide. **Top end — "Appliku" (Python/Django deploy SaaS):** ~10% L→S at top-3 front page (outlier; very strong topic-audience fit). **Bottom end — "ClearNoteLab" (Dec 2025):** zero signups at #28 Show HN. **Median realistic:** front-page Show HN runs 1–3% L→S; below front page collapses fast. **Critical pattern:** HN rewards developer tools / technically-grounded B2B; punishes consumer/marketing framing. Rodix is borderline — "AI memory" reads consumer; "cross-model architectural commitment" reads HN-native. Framing decides which side.

## Source 2 — Product Hunt Top-of-Day (post-2024)

Weakened as one-shot growth event but credible 1,000-visitor day. **Distribution:** ~26.5% of PH launches get 500–1,000 platform users; top-of-day hits 2,000–3,000. **Plausible Analytics, #2 PotD:** 2,399 visitors → ~1.4% trial conversion. **Twinr, #1 PotD:** 1,000 signups launch day, 150/day sustained — but wide-B2C-appeal territory. **Decay:** 80–90% traffic drop within 72 hours. PH is a spike, not a channel.

## Source 3 — IndieHackers as community channel

2024 study across IH postmortems: **IH delivers ~23% conversion per engaged post vs ~3% per PH launch — but only for founders sustaining 4–6 months of presence.** Single IH post from a stranger does little. PostKing: PH+Peerlist combined → 38% of first 100 customers, ~19% L→S off launch traffic.

## Synthesis for Rodix

Three patterns hold. **(1)** Single launch event = one-time spike, not sustained channel; user 101+ depends on content/community/WOM. **(2)** Conversion correlates with topic-audience fit more than channel mechanics — HN-native framing at #5 outperforms generic-SaaS at #1. **(3)** Realistic L→S off launch traffic for indie SaaS with clear wedge sits at **2–4% blended**; HN front-page outliers reach 10%, below-front-page converges on zero. `funnel-model.md` uses 3% blended L→S realistic, 1.5% sensitivity floor.
